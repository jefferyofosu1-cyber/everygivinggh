Step One

Start your fundraiser

The first step in starting your fundraising journey is just that — starting. Once you've created your Every Giving account, your campaign will be live and ready to receive donations in minutes. Here's exactly what to do:

🎯
Set your fundraiser goal
How much are you looking to raise? Be realistic but ambitious. Remember — you can easily change your goal if you meet your initial target. Most successful campaigns on Every Giving set a specific, justified amount.
✍️
Tell your story
Why are you fundraising? Tell your audience what the money is for, who it will help, and why it matters. Be specific — mention names, amounts, and what happens when you reach your goal. Specific stories raise 3× more.
📸
Add a relevant photo or video
A real, authentic photo tells your story better than words. Campaigns with photos raise significantly more than those without. A short video is even better — record yourself explaining why you need help.
🪪
Get verified for maximum trust
Upload your Ghana Card and any supporting documents (hospital bill, school fees letter, etc.). Verified campaigns display a green ✓ badge and raise 3× more than unverified ones. Verification takes just 24 hours.
🚀
Set your campaign live
Once you're happy with everything, hit publish and your campaign goes live instantly. You'll get a shareable link — copy it and you're ready to start sharing with the world.

Step Two

Share with friends — and strangers

An important part of any successful fundraising journey is sharing your page with everyone you can — friends, colleagues, family in the diaspora, church members, neighbours! The more you share, the more you raise. Here's how:

💬
WhatsApp (Most powerful in Ghana)
Send your campaign link to every group chat you're in — family, church, old school, work. WhatsApp is how most Ghanaians share news. A single share in a busy group can bring dozens of donors.
📱 Tap "Share" → WhatsApp
📧
Send emails
Send your campaign link to family and friends with a short personal introduction. A warm, personal email from you will always outperform a generic group message.
✉️ Personal intro works best
📘
Facebook
Post regular updates on your timeline and in relevant Facebook groups. Ask friends to share your post — each share reaches a whole new network of potential donors.
👥 Ask friends to share
📸
Instagram & TikTok
Share your campaign photo as an Instagram story or post. Add the campaign link to your bio. TikTok videos telling your story directly can go viral and reach thousands overnight.
🎥 Video stories work best
📱
SMS & phone calls
Don't underestimate a simple text message or phone call to close family. A personal ask is often more powerful than any social media post. Text your campaign link with a short message.
📲 Direct = most effective
🖨️
Posters with QR code
Print a simple poster with your campaign photo and a QR code linking to your page. Put them up at church, the hospital waiting room, community notice boards, and local shops.
🏷️ Every Giving generates QR free

Say thank you to every donor
Every Giving has built-in thank you tools — tap "Send thanks" next to any donation. A personalised thank you message makes donors feel valued and more likely to share your campaign with others.
📢
Post milestone updates
When you hit 25%, 50%, 75% of your goal — post an update! Celebrate with your donors. Tell them what the progress means and re-energise people who haven't donated yet. Updates appear on your campaign page.
📄
Upload receipts & evidence
When money is used — upload the receipt. Hospital payment confirmation, school fees receipt, building materials invoice. This builds massive trust and often triggers a second wave of donations from people watching.
🙏
Post a final thank you when done
When your campaign ends or goal is met, post a final update with photos and a heartfelt thank you. Donors who gave deserve to know the outcome. This also builds your reputation for future campaigns.
